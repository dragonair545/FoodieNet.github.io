Lets be honest here, sometimes, there is a craving, a craving for that delicious, fluffy, soft naan that you can sadly only find at restaurants. But here's the thing, restaurants are expensive, and you are hungry. But before you look at the recipe, lets have a look at the history of naan.

Naan first came to India in the 1300's from middle eastern travelers. It was commonly eaten with spiced meat, and is now a cusine in places like China, Japan, and Indonesia.

Naan is commonly cooked in a tandoor, a big clay pot, and it is made of a dough and a leavener(But sometimes there is no leavener used) The tandoor and leavener distinguish it from the other popular Indian dish: Roti, which has no leavener and is cooked on a pan. Typically, Naan is used in Indian cusine as something to scoop up other gravies and foods.

Measurements for cooking:

Ingredient Quantity
Oil 1 tbsp
Butter 1 tbsp
Onion(Diced) 1 medium one
Ginger 1 teaspoon(Minced)
Garlic 2 cloves(Crushed)
Chicken Breasts 3 pieces(Cut into 3/4 inch chunks)
Tomato Paste 4 tbsp (1 8oz can)
Garam Masala 1 tbsp
Chili powder 1 teaspoon
Fenugreek 1 teaspoon
Cumin 1 teaspoon
Salt 1 teaspoon
Black Pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Heavy Cream 1 cup

1.Heat a large skillet or medium saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the oil, butter, and onions and cook onions down until lightly golden, about 3-4 minutes. Add ginger and garlic and let it cook for 30 seconds, stirring so it doesn't burn.

2.Add the chicken, tomato paste, and spices. Cook for 5-6 minutes or until everything is cooked through.

3.Add the heavy cream and simmer for 8-10 minutes stirring occasionally. Serve over Basmati rice or with naan.

Tandoori chicken is a dish of chicken marinated in spices and yogurt, which then is cooked in a tandoor, or a clay oven (there are a few steel ones though)

Dishes similar to Tandoori chicken have been found in ancient indian civilizations FIVE FREAKING THOUSAND YEARS AGO! But todays version of tandoori chicken came from the punjab reigon, and was popularized in the late 1940's by the famous New Delhi restaurant: Moti Mahal.

Today, Tandoori Chicken is often served as an appetizer/starter, or sometimes is served with naan to make a main course. Even more, Tandoori Chicken is the base for many other cream-based curries, such as Butter Chicken and Chicken Tiikka

Measurements for cooking:

Ingredient Quantity
Oil 1/4 cup
Green Chillies 2 small chillies
Paprika 1 medium one
Ginger 1 tablespoon(Paste)
Garlic 1 tablespoon(Paste)
Chicken Legs 8 pieces
Coriander 1 tbsp
Garam Masala 1 teaspoon
Chili powder 1 tablespoon
Turmeric 1 teaspoon
Cumin 1 teaspoon
Salt 2 teaspoon
Black Pepper 1/4 teaspoon
Yogurt 1 cup
Lemon 3/4 of a lemon

1. Mix all of the spices together, then in a large bowl, mix 3/4 of the spices, save 1/4 of them, then add the ginger, garlic, chillies(mince them before you add), yogurt, lemon juice, and salt. Whisk the marinade until smooth.

2. Take the chicken legs, remove its skin with a knife, and make a few gashes in it. Then, sprinkle a bit of salt on the chicken, along with the rest 1/4 of the spice mix, before dipping it in the marinade. Put the chicken in a bowl/tray, cover in aluminum foil, then set in the fridge overnight.

3. Preheat your tandoor. Do this by putting a paper bag or handful of newspaper at the bottom, then toss 10 briqquetes(roughly) of charcoal into the tandoor, then light the paper with a match, and the coal should ignite, then, cover the tandoor with a pot lid, but leave some space for gases to excape, then add 5 more pieces every 20 minutes.

4. Hang your chicken on some extra-long skewers, and add a piece of potato about 3 inches from the end to stop them from sliding off. Put them in, and try putting the lid as much as you can, wait about 20 minutes, and you should be done. If you want to check, cut a bit into the meat and if the juices are pink, wait but if it's clear and the meat is white, then you are good. Enjoy with some fresh naan!
